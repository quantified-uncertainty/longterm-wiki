## Overview

This section documents the potential risks from advanced AI systems, organized into four major categories based on the source and nature of the risk.

## Risk Categories

### <EntityLink id="scheming">Accident Risks</EntityLink>
Unintended failures from AI systems pursuing misaligned goals:
- <EntityLink id="scheming">Scheming</EntityLink> - AI strategically concealing misaligned goals
- <EntityLink id="deceptive-alignment">Deceptive Alignment</EntityLink> - Models appearing aligned during training
- <EntityLink id="mesa-optimization">Mesa-Optimization</EntityLink> - Learned optimizers with misaligned objectives
- <EntityLink id="goal-misgeneralization">Goal Misgeneralization</EntityLink> - Objectives that fail in deployment
- <EntityLink id="power-seeking">Power-Seeking</EntityLink> - <EntityLink id="E168">Instrumental convergence</EntityLink> toward acquiring resources

### <EntityLink id="bioweapons">Misuse Risks</EntityLink>
Deliberate harmful applications of AI capabilities:
- <EntityLink id="bioweapons">Bioweapons</EntityLink> - AI-assisted biological weapon development
- <EntityLink id="cyberweapons">Cyberweapons</EntityLink> - Automated cyber attacks and vulnerabilities
- <EntityLink id="disinformation">Disinformation</EntityLink> - Large-scale manipulation campaigns
- <EntityLink id="autonomous-weapons">Autonomous Weapons</EntityLink> - Lethal autonomous systems

### <EntityLink id="racing-dynamics">Structural Risks</EntityLink>
Systemic issues from how AI development is organized:
- <EntityLink id="racing-dynamics">Racing Dynamics</EntityLink> - Competitive pressure reducing safety investment
- <EntityLink id="concentration-of-power">Concentration of Power</EntityLink> - Dangerous accumulation of AI capabilities
- <EntityLink id="lock-in">Lock-in</EntityLink> - Irreversible entrenchment of values or structures
- <EntityLink id="economic-disruption">Economic Disruption</EntityLink> - Labor market and economic instability

### <EntityLink id="trust-decline">Epistemic Risks</EntityLink>
Threats to society's ability to know and reason:
- <EntityLink id="trust-decline">Trust Decline</EntityLink> - Erosion of institutional and interpersonal trust
- <EntityLink id="authentication-collapse">Authentication Collapse</EntityLink> - Inability to verify authentic content
- <EntityLink id="expertise-atrophy">Expertise Atrophy</EntityLink> - Loss of human capability through AI dependence

## How Risks Connect

Many risks interact and compound. For example:
- <EntityLink id="E239">Racing dynamics</EntityLink> → reduced safety testing → higher accident risk
- <EntityLink id="E102">Disinformation</EntityLink> → <EntityLink id="E362">trust decline</EntityLink> → reduced <EntityLink id="E76">coordination capacity</EntityLink>
- Power concentration → <EntityLink id="E189">lock-in</EntityLink> potential → governance failures

See the <EntityLink id="risk-interaction-matrix">Risk Interaction Matrix</EntityLink> for detailed analysis.
